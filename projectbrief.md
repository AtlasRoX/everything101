# Everything101

Everything101 is an open-source knowledge platform designed to make learning any subject fast, simple, and enjoyable. 

Our mission is to explain the world's most important ideas—spanning technology, science, philosophy, finance, history, psychology, art, and daily life—in a friendly way that anyone can understand and grab the concepts within minutes.

Unlike Wikipedia (which prioritizes exhaustive completeness) or textbooks (which assume prior specialized knowledge), Everything101 prioritizes absolute clarity. Every article is built from first principles, starting with zero assumptions about the reader's background, so that they walk away with a genuine "Aha!" moment rather than more confusion.

---

## 1. Vision & Core Philosophy

### Vision
We want to create a place where anyone can search for a topic and genuinely understand it in a single sitting. Whether searching for:
- Python
- Linux
- Inflation
- Black Holes
- Democracy
- DNA
- Artificial Intelligence
- Quantum Computing
- Docker
- Calculus
- Philosophy
- Blockchain
- Music Theory
- Evolution

The goal is to ensure the reader walks away with a clear, functional understanding of the topic, feeling confident and inspired to learn more.

### Mission & Core Philosophy
- **Reduce the Barrier to Learning:** Make high-quality, comprehensive education completely free.
- **De-clutter Complexity:** Complexity should come from the topic itself, never from poor explanation. We remove academic filler and use simple, active language.
- **Explain from First Principles:** Explain how things work, why they exist, and what they actually do before throwing definitions at the reader.
- **Teach Beginners, Don't Impress Experts:** Every article must optimize for immediate understanding rather than showing off technical expertise.

### Value Proposition
Everything101 is not a simple directory of links, an awesome-list, a textbook, or a replica of Wikipedia. It is a **knowledge compression engine**. We structure and compress knowledge so that readers can understand in 5 to 15 minutes what would normally take hours of searching across disjointed websites.

## 2. Target Audience & Engagement

Everything101 is tailored for anyone asking: *"I've heard of this concept before... but what actually is it, and how does it work?"* 

Our readers include:
- Complete beginners exploring a topic for the first time.
- Students needing a clear, intuitive introduction.
- Self-directed learners and curious minds.
- Professionals and developers entering a new domain.
- Teachers looking for simple analogies and structures to explain ideas to their students.
- Researchers seeking a quick, clean refresher.

---

## 3. Editorial & Writing Guidelines

### Core Principles
Every single article must be:
- **Accurate & Fact-Checked:** Built on verified, source-backed information.
- **Beginner-Friendly:** Assuming no prior specialized background.
- **Concise & Skimmable:** Short sentences, short paragraphs, and clear subheadings.
- **Practical:** Packed with real-world examples and interactive analogies.
- **Free Forever:** Open-source and accessible to everyone.

Every sentence should reduce confusion, every paragraph should answer a clear question, and every diagram should simplify a difficult concept.

### Writing Style
- Use simple English and avoid unnecessary technical jargon.
- Always explain a concept using simple terms before introducing formal terminology.
- Use relatable real-world analogies (e.g., explaining Docker as shipping containers for software).
- Avoid filler words, passive voice, and repetitive phrasing.
- Optimize the layout for visual readability with bullet points, bold highlights, and code blocks.

### The "Aha!" Quality Standard
A reader should understand the core idea after reading the article once. If a beginner finishes an article still feeling lost or confused, then the article is incomplete and needs to be rewritten.

## 4. Scope & Content Domains

To ensure Everything101 is organized and scalable, our articles are grouped into five major themed clusters:

- **Technology & Engineering:** Programming (Python, etc.), Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Operating Systems (Linux, Windows), Databases, Networking, Cybersecurity, Cloud Computing, Electronics, Robotics, and the Internet.
- **Sciences & Mathematics:** Mathematics, Calculus, Statistics, Data Science, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Medicine, and Astronomy.
- **Humanities, Finance & Society:** Economics, Finance, Business, Psychology, Philosophy, History, Politics, Law, and Geography.
- **Arts, Design & Communication:** Art, Music, Music Theory, Architecture, Design, Writing, and Communication.
- **Daily Life & Personal Growth:** Career, Productivity, Learning, Languages, Research, Health, Environment, and any other field worth learning.

## 5. Operations & Repository Structure

### Directory Architecture
The repository is designed to scale to thousands of topics without clutter:
- Every major content category has a dedicated parent directory.
- Every topic resides in its own dedicated subfolder containing:
  - `README.md` (the main article)
  - `assets/` (images, diagrams, references, and other assets)
- No oversized directories or flat, unorganized markdown files are allowed. Everything must remain searchable, neat, and highly structured.

### Contribution Philosophy
We welcome contributions from anyone! However, adding more information is not always an improvement. Every contribution must improve clarity. Removing confusion is our highest metric of success.

### References & Sources Policy
Every article must include a list of trusted references to support its claims:
- Official documentation (where applicable)
- Textbooks and academic research papers
- Educational resources and reliable websites
- Historical sources and original documents
- No unsupported or speculative claims are permitted.

## 6. Project Roadmap

### Phase 1: GitHub & Markdown Foundation
- Establish the open-source GitHub repository.
- Establish editorial guidelines and workflows.
- Launch the initial set of core articles built with community contributions.

### Phase 2: Web Platform & Navigation
- Build a fast, lightweight, and modern website.
- Implement search functionality and category browsing.
- Integrate reading progress tracking, dark mode, and seamless navigation.

### Phase 3: Interactive Learning & AI Search
- Introduce interactive diagrams and visual simulations.
- Build a visual knowledge graph and personalized learning paths.
- Embed quizzes to reinforce concepts.
- Deploy semantic AI search.

### Phase 4: Native Applications & Global Outreach
- Release native mobile applications (iOS and Android).
- Build a helpful AI-powered tutor to answer clarifying questions.
- Support community-led global translations.
- Support offline reading and expose a developer API.

## 7. Long-Term Goal

Our long-term goal is to become the world's most trusted, beginner-friendly knowledge repository. We aim to be the very first place anyone—from a curious child to a software engineer—visits when they want to learn something new. 

Everything101 will contain thousands of carefully curated articles across all domains of human knowledge, held together by one uncompromising standard: **If it cannot be understood by a beginner, it does not belong in Everything101.**
