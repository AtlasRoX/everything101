# Family Law 101

> *Family law is a legal practice area that focuses on issues involving family relationships, such as adoption, divorce, child custody, and domestic partnerships, as well as estate inheritance.*

---

## The Big Picture

Why is the state involved in our personal relationships? 
- We do not ask the government for permission to make friends or start dating. 
- However, when people get married, have children, buy homes together, or pass away, their personal lives create complex financial and legal obligations.

Family law provides the legal framework to manage these obligations. 

It defines what marriage is, how assets are split when relationships end, how children are protected, and how property is distributed after death. 

Because family law deals with highly emotional personal issues, courts try to balance legal rules with equity (fairness) and the "best interests" of vulnerable parties.

This article introduces the marriage contract, the divorce process, child custody, and the basics of wills and inheritance.

---

## Marriage and the Prenuptial Agreement

In the eyes of the law, **marriage is a legal contract** that automatically triggers specific rights and duties:
- Joint tax filing options.
- Medical decision-making rights if a spouse is incapacitated.
- Shared property ownership rights.
- Right to inherit a spouse's assets automatically.

### Prenuptial Agreement (Prenup):
A contract signed by a couple *before* marriage that determines how their individual assets will be split if they get divorced. 
- **Why?** It overrides the default state divorce laws, allowing couples to protect pre-marriage wealth or family businesses.

## Divorce and Custody

When a marriage contract is legally dissolved, the court must resolve three primary issues:

```
                            ┌─────────────────────────┐
                            │    DIVORCE ACTIONS      │
                            └────────────┬────────────┘
                                         │
         ┌───────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────┐
         ▼                               ▼                               ▼
┌──────────────────┐            ┌──────────────────┐            ┌──────────────────┐
│ PROPERTY DIVISION│            │  ALIMONY (SPOUSE)│            │  CHILD CUSTODY   │
│ Split assets     │            │ Financial support│            │ Custody & support│
│ (Equitable or    │            │ paid to lower-   │            │ based on child's │
│ community prop.) │            │ earning spouse   │            │ "best interests" │
└──────────────────┘            └──────────────────┘            └──────────────────┘
```

1. **Property Division:** 
  - **Community Property States:** All assets acquired during the marriage are owned 50/50 and split equally.
  - **Equitable Distribution States:** Assets are split "fairly" based on the judge's assessment of each spouse's needs and contributions (not always 50/50).
2. **Spousal Support (Alimony):** Ongoing payments made by the higher-earning spouse to support the other, helping them maintain their standard of living post-divorce.
3. **Child Custody & Support:**
  - **Legal Custody:** The right to make major decisions about the child's life (schooling, religion, medical care).
  - **Physical Custody:** Who the child physically lives with.
  - **The Standard:** Courts determine custody based entirely on the **"best interests of the child"** standard, ignoring the parents' selfish desires.

## Wills and Inheritance: Distributing Estates

What happens to your property when you die?
- **Will:** A legal document stating how you want your assets distributed and who should care for your minor children after your death.
- **Probate:** The court-supervised process of proving a will is valid, paying off the deceased person's debts, and distributing the remaining assets.
- **Intestate:** Dying without a will. If you die intestate, the state uses a default legal formula to distribute your assets to your closest living relatives (spouse, children, parents), which might not align with your true wishes.

---

## Key Terms

- **Family Law:** The branch of law governing domestic relations and estates.
- **Prenuptial Agreement:** A pre-marriage asset division contract.
- **Alimony:** Spousal support payments.
- **Custody:** Legal and physical responsibility for a child.
- **Will:** A legal document detailing asset distribution after death.
- **Probate:** The legal process validating a will.
- **Intestate:** The condition of dying without a valid will.
