# Migration 101

> *Migration is the permanent or semi-permanent relocation of an individual or group from one location to another.*

---

## The Big Picture

Humans have always been on the move.
- Thousands of years ago, we migrated out of Africa, spreading across land bridges to populate the continents. 
- Today, hundreds of millions of people live in a country different from the one they were born in.

Migration is not random. It is a spatial response to opportunity and danger. 

Whether it is a software engineer moving from Bangalore to Silicon Valley for a higher salary, or a family fleeing a civil war zone in Syria, people move to balance their lives against geography.

This article introduces push and pull factors, the distinction between voluntary and forced migration, and legal classifications of migrants.

---

## Push vs. Pull Factors

Why do people leave their homes? Geographers divide the reasons into **Push Factors** (negative things driving you away) and **Pull Factors** (positive things drawing you in):

```
                       ┌─────────────────────────┐
                       │    MIGRATION FORCES     │
                       └────────────┬────────────┘
                                    │
         ┌──────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────┐
         ▼                                                     ▼
┌──────────────────┐                                  ┌──────────────────┐
│   PUSH FACTORS   │                                  │   PULL FACTORS   │
│ (Negative Force) │                                  │ (Positive Force) │
│ - High unemployment                                  │ - Job vacancies  │
│ - War, violence  │                                  │ - Safety, peace  │
│ - Famine, drought│                                  │ - Good schools   │
│ - Persecution    │                                  │ - Freedom        │
└──────────────────┘                                  └──────────────────┘
```

These forces operate across four domains:
- **Economic (The Primary Driver):** Searching for work, higher wages, or career advancement.
- **Political:** Fleeing corrupt governments, persecution, or military conflict.
- **Environmental:** Fleeing natural disasters, soil erosion, or rising sea levels. (See [ClimateMigration101](./ClimateMigration101.md)).
- **Social/Cultural:** Moving to join family members who migrated earlier (**Chain Migration**).

---

## Voluntary vs. Forced Migration

- **Voluntary Migration:** The migrant chose to move, usually for economic reasons, weighing the opportunities against the cost of leaving.
- **Forced Migration:** The migrant was compelled to move due to cultural, political, or environmental factors that threatened their survival. (e.g., the transatlantic slave trade, or fleeing active warfare).

---

## Legal Classifications of Forced Migrants

Under international law, forced migrants are classified into three distinct categories, which determine their rights:

1. **Refugee:** A person who has been forced to flee their country because of a well-founded fear of persecution, war, or violence. Refugees are protected by international law and cannot be returned to a country where their life is in danger.
2. **Asylum Seeker:** Someone who has migrated to another country in the hope of being recognized as a refugee, but whose legal claim has not yet been processed.
3. **Internally Displaced Person (IDP):** Someone who was forced to flee their home for the same reasons as a refugee, but who **remains inside their own country's borders**. IDPs do not receive the same international legal protections as refugees because they are still under their own sovereign government's authority.

---

## Key Terms

- **Migration:** Permanent relocation to a new home.
- **Emigration:** Leaving your home country (out-migration).
- **Immigration:** Entering a new country (in-migration).
- **Push Factor:** A negative condition encouraging emigration.
- **Pull Factor:** A positive condition encouraging immigration.
- **Chain Migration:** Migration of people to a specific location because relatives previously settled there.
- **Refugee:** A forced migrant fleeing international persecution.
- **IDP:** An internally displaced person fleeing home but remaining in-country.
