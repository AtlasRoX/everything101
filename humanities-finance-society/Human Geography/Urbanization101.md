# Urbanization 101

> *Urbanization is the process by which large numbers of people become permanently concentrated in relatively small areas, forming cities.*

---

## The Big Picture

In 1800, only 3% of the world's population lived in cities.
- By 1950, it was 30%.
- Today, over 55% of humanity lives in urban areas, and that number is projected to hit 68% by 2050.

We are living in the age of the city. 

**Urbanization** is one of the most powerful geographical shifts in human history. It changes how we work, how we travel, and how we consume resources. However, rapid urban growth also creates immense challenges: from traffic congestion and air pollution to housing crises and the rise of massive informal settlements (slums).

This article introduces the classification of giant cities, the phenomenon of urban sprawl, and modern sustainable design solutions.

---

## Megacities vs. Metacities

As cities swell, they reach unprecedented sizes. Geographers use population thresholds to classify these massive urban clusters:

- **Megacities:** Urban areas with a total population of **10 million or more** people. (e.g., New York, Cairo, Lagos).
- **Metacities:** Agglomerations with a total population of **20 million or more** people. (e.g., Tokyo, Delhi, São Paulo).
- **Tokyo's Scale:** Tokyo, Japan, is the world's largest metacity, packing over 37 million people into its metropolitan area—more than the entire population of Canada.

---

## The Suburbs & Urban Sprawl

In developed nations (especially the US), the post-WWII era saw a migration *out* of city centers:

```
                            ┌─────────────────────────┐
                            │    THE SPATIAL SHIFT    │
                            └────────────┬────────────┘
                                         │
         ┌───────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────┐
         ▼                                                               ▼
┌──────────────────┐                                            ┌──────────────────┐
│   CITY CENTER    │                                            │   THE SUBURBS    │
│ High density     │                                            │ Low density      │
│ Mixed use        │                                            │ Single-use zoning│
│ Walkable         │                                            │ Auto-dependent   │
└──────────────────┘                                            └──────────────────┘
```

- **Suburbanization:** The movement of upper and middle-class populations from core urban areas to the outskirts. (Driven by cheap land, highway construction, and single-family home desires).
- **Urban Sprawl:** The rapid, unrestricted expansion of housing and commercial development across large areas of rural land, with low-density residential zoning.
- *The Problem:* Sprawl forces car dependency, increases air pollution, and consumes valuable agricultural land.

---

## Urban Challenges: Slums and Gentrification

- **Informal Settlements (Slums/Favelas):** Residential areas where housing is built illegally on land without land titles, lacking basic public services like clean water, electricity, sanitation, or paved roads. (Common in rapidly urbanizing developing nations).
- **Gentrification:** The process of converting an urban neighborhood from a low-income area to a middle-to-high-income area. While it improves property values and infrastructure, it often displaces long-term, low-income residents who can no longer afford the rising rent.

---

## Sustainable Urbanism: Smart Growth

To combat sprawl and pollution, urban planners advocate for **Smart Growth** (or New Urbanism):
- **Walkability:** Designing neighborhoods where daily needs are within a 15-minute walk.
- **Mixed-Use Development:** Combining residential, commercial, and green spaces in the same building or neighborhood.
- **Public Transit:** Prioritizing subways, trains, and bike lanes over personal cars.

---

## Key Terms

- **Urbanization:** The concentration of populations into cities.
- **Megacity:** A city with over 10 million residents.
- **Metacity:** A city with over 20 million residents.
- **Urban Sprawl:** Low-density, automobile-dependent city expansion.
- **Slum:** An informal urban settlement lacking basic infrastructure.
- **Gentrification:** The displacement of low-income residents by wealthier buyers.
