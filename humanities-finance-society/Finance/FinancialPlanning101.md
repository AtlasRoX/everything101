# Financial Planning 101

> *Financial planning is a comprehensive evaluation of an individual's current and future financial state by using currently known variables to predict future cash flows, asset values, and withdrawal plans.*

---

## The Big Picture

If you were going to build a house, you wouldn't start by buying wood and hammering nails randomly. You would hire an architect to draw a blueprint.

Yet, many people manage their money with no plan. They save a little, spend a little, and hope that somehow they will have enough money to buy a house, put their children through college, and retire comfortably.

**Financial Planning** is the blueprint for your money. It takes your vague life goals ("I want to buy a house someday") and translates them into specific, mathematical steps ("I need to save $1,200 a month for 4 years to afford a 20% down payment").

This article introduces the planning process, the timeline of financial goals, and the importance of hiring a fiduciary advisor.

## The 6-Step Financial Planning Process

A professional financial plan follows a systematic cycle:

```
┌──────────────┐      ┌──────────────┐      ┌──────────────┐      ┌──────────────┐
│  1. STATUS   │─────►│  2. GOALS    │─────►│ 3. ANALYZE   │─────►│  4. DEVELOP  │
│ Audit net    │      │ Define SMART │      │ Find gaps &  │      │ Write custom │
│ worth/cash   │      │ financial    │      │ shortfalls   │      │ action steps │
└──────────────┘      │ objectives   │      └──────────────┘      └──────┬───────┘
                      └──────────────┘                                   │
                                                                         ▼
┌──────────────┐                                                  ┌──────────────┐
│  6. MONITOR  │◄─────────────────────────────────────────────────│ 5. IMPLEMENT │
│ Annual audit │                                                  │ Put plan in  │
│ & adjustment │                                                  │ motion       │
└──────────────┘                                                  └──────────────┘
```

1. **Assess Current Status:** Calculate your net worth statement and track your monthly cash flow. (See [PersonalFinance101](./PersonalFinance101.md)).
2. **Identify Goals:** Write down your objectives using the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
3. **Analyze Gaps:** Check if your current savings rate is high enough to reach those goals. If you need $100,000 in 5 years, but are only saving $500 a month, you have a gap.
4. **Develop the Plan:** Design specific strategies to close the gap (e.g., cutting expenses, changing asset allocation, or taking a side job).
5. **Implement the Plan:** Put the plan in motion (e.g., setting up automatic monthly transfers to an investment account).
6. **Monitor and Update:** Review the plan at least once a year or after major life events (marriage, baby, promotion) and adjust the course.

## Timeline of Goals: Cash vs. Investing

Different goals require different financial tools, depending on when you need the money:

- **Short-Term Goals (0 to 2 years):** Buying a car, going on a vacation, or building an emergency fund.
  - *Strategy:* Keep the money in HYSAs or CDs. Do not invest this money in stocks, as a market crash could wipe out your savings just before you need it. (See [Saving101](./Saving101.md)).
- **Medium-Term Goals (2 to 7 years):** Buying a house or starting a business.
  - *Strategy:* Use a mix of high-yield savings and conservative investments (like short-term bonds or balanced ETFs).
- **Long-Term Goals (7+ years):** Retirement or college funding for a newborn.
  - *Strategy:* Invest aggressively in stocks and index funds, as you have time to ride out short-term market crashes and benefit from compound interest. (See [Investing101](./Investing101.md)).

## Choosing an Advisor: The Fiduciary Standard

If you choose to hire a professional to help build your plan, you must check their legal status. Look for a **Fiduciary**:

- **Fiduciary Advisor:** Legally obligated to act in your best financial interest. If a product is bad for you but pays the advisor a high commission, a fiduciary is legally banned from selling it to you. (Usually charges a flat fee or hourly rate).
- **Non-Fiduciary Broker:** Only held to a "suitability" standard. They can sell you high-fee, mediocre investments because they are "suitable," even if cheaper, better options exist, to earn a commission for themselves.

---

## Key Terms

- **Financial Planning:** Formulating a comprehensive plan to meet personal goals.
- **SMART Goals:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound objectives.
- **Fiduciary:** A legal obligation to act in the best interest of another party.
- **Net Worth Statement:** A listing of personal assets and liabilities.
