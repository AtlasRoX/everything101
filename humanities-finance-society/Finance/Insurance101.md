# Insurance 101

> *Insurance is a contract, represented by a policy, in which an individual or entity receives financial protection or reimbursement against losses from an insurance company. It is the primary tool for risk transfer.*

---

## The Big Picture

Imagine you spent ten years saving $300,000 to buy a house. 
- The day after you move in, a fire breaks out and burns the house to the ground. 
- In a single day, your entire net worth is wiped out, and you are left homeless and bankrupt.

To prevent this catastrophe, you buy **insurance**. 

Insurance is a mechanism of **risk transfer**. Instead of carrying the massive, unpredictable risk of your house burning down yourself, you pay a small, predictable fee (a premium) to an insurance company. The insurance company pools your fee with thousands of other homeowners. Because only a few houses will actually burn down in any given year, the pool has enough money to rebuild your house if a fire occurs.

This article introduces the core variables of an insurance policy, the different types of insurance, and the concept of risk management.

---

## Core Insurance Variables: Premium vs. Deductible

Every insurance contract (policy) has three key terms you must understand:

- **Premium:** The monthly or annual fee you pay to the insurance company to keep your policy active.
- **Deductible:** The out-of-pocket amount you must pay yourself before the insurance company pays anything.
- **Policy Limit:** The maximum amount the insurance company will pay out for a loss covered under the policy.

> **The Trade-off:** Premiums and deductibles have an inverse relationship. If you choose a **high deductible** (promising to pay the first $2,000 of a car crash yourself), the insurance company carries less risk, so they will lower your monthly **premium**. If you choose a **low deductible** ($250), your monthly **premium** will be high.

## Four Essential Types of Insurance

For a robust financial plan, individuals typically need four types of insurance to cover catastrophic risks:

```
                            ┌─────────────────────────┐
                            │    ESSENTIAL INSURANCE  │

│ Covers medical ││ Covers car   ││ Rebuilds your││ Pays family  ││ Replaces wage│

│ surgeries      ││ liability    ││ fire/storms  ││ away         ││ injured      │
└────────────────┘└──────────────┘└──────────────┘└──────────────┘└──────────────┘
```

1. **Health Insurance:** Covers medical expenses, hospital stays, and prescription drugs. (Essential because medical bills are the leading cause of personal bankruptcy in many nations).
2. **Auto Insurance:** Covers damage to your vehicle, and more importantly, **liability**—the cost of damage or injuries you cause to others in a crash.
3. **Property (Homeowners/Renters) Insurance:** Rebuilds your home and replaces your possessions after a fire, theft, or natural disaster.
4. **Life Insurance:** Pays a cash sum (death benefit) to your family (beneficiaries) if you die, replacing your income so they can pay the mortgage and buy groceries.
5. **Disability Insurance:** Replaces a portion of your income if you are injured or sick and unable to work for months or years (protecting your human capital).

## Risk Management: Avoid, Reduce, Assume, or Transfer

Insurance is only one tool in the broader science of **risk management**. When facing a risk, you have four choices:

- **Avoid:** Eliminate the risk by not participating. (e.g., avoiding skydiving).
- **Reduce:** Lower the probability of the risk occurring. (e.g., wearing a seatbelt, installing fire alarms in your house).
- **Assume (Retain):** Accept the risk yourself because the cost is small. (e.g., not buying a warranty for a $20 toaster).
- **Transfer:** Pay someone else to carry the risk. (e.g., buying home insurance).

---

## Key Terms

- **Insurance:** A contract transferring risk from an individual to an entity.
- **Premium:** The regular payment made to keep insurance active.
- **Deductible:** The out-of-pocket payment made by the policyholder before insurance kicks in.
- **Liability:** Legal responsibility for damage or injury caused to others.
- **Beneficiary:** The person designated to receive funds from a life insurance policy.
