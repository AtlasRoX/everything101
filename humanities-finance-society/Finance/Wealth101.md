# Wealth 101

> *Wealth is the abundance of valuable financial assets or physical possessions which can be converted into a form that can be used for transactions. In personal finance, it represents the degree of financial independence.*

---

## The Big Picture

What does it mean to be wealthy?
- Many people think wealth is measured by what you show the world: a luxury car, a mansion, or designer clothes. 

But in finance, this is a confusion of **income** and **wealth**. If you make $500,000 a year but spend $500,000 a year on high-end consumption, you have a high income, but your wealth is zero. In fact, you are highly fragile; if you lose your job next week, you will go bankrupt.

True wealth is **invisible**. It is the assets you have saved and invested that have *not* been spent on consumption. 

As the author Morgan Housel wrote: **"Wealth is the nice cars not purchased. The diamonds not bought. The watches not worn. Wealth is financial assets that haven't yet been converted into the stuff you see."**

This article introduces the distinction between assets and liabilities, the power of passive income, and the meaning of financial independence.

---

## Assets vs. Liabilities: The Wealth Builder's View

In his famous personal finance book *Rich Dad Poor Dad*, Robert Kiyosaki simplified the accounting definitions of assets and liabilities to explain how wealth is built:

```
               ┌───────────────────────────────┐
               │         THE WEALTH FORK       │
               └──────────────┬────────────────┘
                              │
         ┌────────────────────┴────────────────────┐
         ▼                                         ▼
┌──────────────────┐                      ┌──────────────────┐
│   TRUE ASSETS    │                      │ TRUE LIABILITIES │
│ Put money INTO   │                      │ Take money OUT   │
│ your pocket      │                      │ of your pocket   │
│ (Rental property,│                      │ (Credit cards,   │
│  stocks, bonds)  │                      │  car loans, rent)│
└──────────────────┘                      └──────────────────┘
```

- **True Assets:** Things that put money *into* your pocket. Examples: dividend-paying stocks, rental real estate, bonds that pay interest, or intellectual property royalties.
- **True Liabilities:** Things that take money *out* of your pocket. Examples: credit card balances, car loans, club memberships, and rent.

To become wealthy, you must focus your life on **buying assets that generate income**, rather than buying liabilities that require maintenance costs.

## Passive Income: Letting Money Work for You

Most people make money through **active income**—trading their time for a wage. (If you don't work, you don't get paid). (See [Labor101](../Economics/Labor101.md)).

Wealthy people focus on building **passive income**—money earned from assets that does not require active, daily labor to maintain:

- **Dividends:** Quarterly payouts from stocks you own.
- **Interest:** Income from bonds or savings products.
- **Rental Income:** Monthly rent collected from tenants.
- **Royalties:** Income from books, software, or patents you created once.

Once your passive income exceeds your monthly living expenses, you have achieved **financial independence**. You no longer *have* to work to survive; you are free to spend your time however you choose.

---

## Key Terms

- **Wealth:** The accumulation of assets that maintain or grow value over time.
- **Active Income:** Income earned from direct labor (e.g., salary).
- **Passive Income:** Income generated automatically by assets without active daily work.
- **Financial Independence:** The state of having sufficient personal wealth to live without having to work actively for basic necessities.
- **Depreciating Asset:** An item that loses value over time (e.g., a new car).
