# Risk 101

> *In finance, risk is the probability that an investment's actual return will differ from its expected return, including the possibility of losing some or all of the original investment.*

---

## The Big Picture

Many people refuse to invest because they are afraid of **risk**. They saw stock markets crash in 2008 or 2020 and worry that their savings could disappear overnight.

But in finance, risk is not something to be feared; it is something to be **measured and managed**. 

Without risk, there are no returns. The key to successful investing is to understand the different types of risk you are taking, and to use strategies like diversification to eliminate the risks that do not pay you back, while accepting the risks that do.

This article introduces systematic vs. unsystematic risk, other hidden risks like inflation and liquidity, and how to manage them.

---

## Systematic vs. Unsystematic Risk: The Core Split

Economists divide investment risk into two main categories:

```
                            ┌─────────────────────────┐
                            │    INVESTMENT RISKS     │
                            └────────────┬────────────┘
                                         │
         ┌───────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────┐
         ▼                                                               ▼
┌──────────────────┐                                            ┌──────────────────┐
│ SYSTEMATIC RISK  │                                            │UNSYSTEMATIC RISK │
│ (Market Risk)    │                                            │ (Specific Risk)  │
│ - Affects whole  │                                            │ - Affects only   │
│   economy        │                                            │   one company    │
│ - Cannot be      │                                            │ - Can be easily  │
│   diversified    │                                            │   diversified    │
└──────────────────┘                                            └──────────────────┘
```

### 1. Systematic Risk (Market Risk)
Risks that affect the entire financial market or economy simultaneously.
- *Examples:* Recessions, pandemics, interest rate hikes, wars, or general economic downturns.
- *Nature:* You cannot escape systematic risk by buying different stocks. If the entire market drops (like in 2020), almost all stocks fall. Investors are rewarded with higher returns over the long run *specifically* for carrying systematic risk.

### 2. Unsystematic Risk (Specific Risk)
Risks that affect only a single company or industry.
- *Examples:* A pharmaceutical company's new drug is rejected by regulators; a tech company's factory burns down; or a CEO is caught in a scandal.
- *Nature:* You can completely eliminate unsystematic risk through **diversification**—by owning 500 different stocks instead of just one. If you only own one stock and it goes bankrupt, you lose everything. If you own 500 stocks and one goes bankrupt, your portfolio barely notices.

## Other Key Financial Risks

Investors must watch out for three other hidden risks:

- **Inflation Risk (Purchasing Power Risk):** The risk that your investment returns do not keep up with inflation. (e.g., keeping cash in a 1% savings account while inflation is 3%).
- **Default (Credit) Risk:** The risk that a borrower fails to pay back their debt. This is the primary risk of bonds. (Governments have low default risk; startup businesses have high default risk).
- **Liquidity Risk:** The risk that you cannot sell an asset quickly for its fair market value when you need cash. (e.g., physical real estate has high liquidity risk, while public stocks have low liquidity risk).

---

## How to Manage Risk: Diversification

The golden rule of risk management is **"Don't put all your eggs in one basket."**

By diversifying across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) and different geographies (US, Europe, Asia), you protect your portfolio from specific sectoral failures, ensuring steady, long-term growth. (See [Portfolio101](./Portfolio101.md)).

---

## Key Terms

- **Risk:** The uncertainty of investment returns.
- **Systematic Risk:** Economy-wide risk that cannot be diversified away.
- **Unsystematic Risk:** Company-specific risk that can be eliminated by diversification.
- **Inflation Risk:** The risk of cash losing purchasing power over time.
- **Default Risk:** The probability that a borrower fails to meet debt payments.
- **Liquidity Risk:** The difficulty of converting an asset into cash quickly.
