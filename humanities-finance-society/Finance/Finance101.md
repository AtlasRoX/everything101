# Finance 101

> *Finance is the study and system of money, investments, and other financial instruments. It is the science of allocating assets and liabilities over time under conditions of certainty and uncertainty.*

---

## The Big Picture

What is the difference between economics and finance?
- **Economics** is the broad study of scarcity, choices, and how societies allocate resources. (See [Economics101](../Economics/Economics101.md)).
- **Finance** is the practical application. It is the specific study of how individuals, companies, and governments manage money, raise capital, evaluate investments, and mitigate risk over time.

At its heart, finance is about **time** and **uncertainty**. Every financial decision involves a trade-off: *Should I spend $1,000 today, or save it to have more in the future? If I lend this money, will I get it back?* 

Finance provides the mathematical and organizational tools to price these trade-offs, value future cash flows, and manage risks.

This article introduces the three main branches of finance, the concept of the time value of money, and the relationship between risk and reward.

How individuals and families manage their budgets, save for emergencies, invest for the future, buy insurance, and plan for retirement. (See [PersonalFinance101](./PersonalFinance101.md)).

## The Time Value of Money (TVM)

The most fundamental rule of finance is the **Time Value of Money**:

> **Time Value of Money (TVM):** A dollar in your hand today is worth more than a dollar to be received in the future.

This is true for three reasons:
1. **Opportunity Cost (Interest):** If you have a dollar today, you can invest it and earn interest, turning it into more than a dollar in the future.
2. **Inflation:** Over time, inflation raises prices, meaning a dollar in the future will buy fewer goods than a dollar today. (See [Inflation101](../Economics/Inflation101.md)).
3. **Risk:** A dollar in your hand today is guaranteed; a dollar promised in the future carries a risk that the sender might go bankrupt and fail to pay you.

---

## Risk and Reward: The Financial Trade-off

In finance, you cannot get high returns without taking high risks. This is the **risk-return trade-off**:
- If you want absolute safety, you must accept low returns (e.g., a government savings account earning 2% interest).
- If you want high returns (e.g., doubling your money in a year), you must accept a high risk of losing your capital (e.g., investing in a startup or cryptocurrency).

---

## Key Terms

- **Finance:** The system of money management, lending, and investment.
- **Time Value of Money:** The concept that money available now is worth more than the same amount in the future.
- **Assets:** Resources that have economic value (e.g., cash, stocks, real estate).
- **Liabilities:** Financial debts or obligations owed to others (e.g., loans, credit card balances).
- **Risk-Return Trade-off:** The principle that potential return rises with an increase in risk.
