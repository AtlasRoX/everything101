# Return 101

> *In finance, return is the financial gain or loss on an investment over a specified period, expressed as a percentage of the investment's initial cost.*

---

## The Big Picture

If you buy a stock for $100 and sell it for $110, you made $10. Was that a good return? 
- If you made that $10 in **one week**, it is an incredible return. 
- If you made that $10 over **ten years**, it is a terrible return because you failed to beat inflation and lost opportunity costs.

To evaluate investments, you must know how to calculate and compare **returns**. 

Returns are not just about the change in price of an asset (capital gains); they also include any cash the asset pays you while you own it (like stock dividends or bond coupons). Comparing these returns on an annualized basis is the only way to make smart portfolio decisions.

This article introduces ROI, capital gains vs. yield, annualized returns, and real vs. nominal returns.

---

## The Core Metric: Return on Investment (ROI)

The simplest way to measure the performance of an investment is **Return on Investment (ROI)**:

$$\text{ROI} = \frac{\text{Current Value} - \text{Initial Cost}}{\text{Initial Cost}} \times 100$$

- **Example:** You buy shares for **$1,000** and sell them for **$1,250**.
  - $\text{ROI} = \frac{1,250 - 1,000}{1,000} \times 100 = 25\%$.
- **The Limit:** ROI does not account for time. A 25% return over 1 year is great; a 25% return over 20 years is very poor.

## The Two Sources of Return: Capital Gains vs. Yield

When you invest in an asset, you make money in two distinct ways:

```
                            ┌─────────────────────────┐
                            │    INVESTMENT RETURNS   │
                            └────────────┬────────────┘
                                         │
         ┌───────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────┐
         ▼                                                               ▼
┌──────────────────┐                                            ┌──────────────────┐
│  CAPITAL GAINS   │                                            │  YIELD (INCOME)  │
│ (Price Increase) │                                            │ (Cash Payments)  │
│ Buy low, sell    │                                            │ Cash paid while  │
│ high (Unrealized │                                            │ you hold asset   │
│ until sold)      │                                            │ (Dividends, rent)│
└──────────────────┘                                            └──────────────────┘
```

### 1. Capital Gains
The increase in the market price of an asset.
- **Unrealized Gain:** Your stock price rose from $100 to $120, but you haven't sold it yet. (Paper wealth).
- **Realized Gain:** You actually sell the stock, locking in your $20 profit. (Subject to capital gains tax).

### 2. Yield (Income Return)
The cash payouts an asset generates while you hold it, expressed as an annual percentage of the asset's price.
- **Dividend Yield (Stocks):** Annual Dividends per Share / Stock Price. (e.g., a stock worth $100 paying $3 in annual dividends has a 3% yield).
- **Current Yield (Bonds):** Annual Interest Payment / Bond Price.
- **Rental Yield (Real Estate):** Annual Rent / Property Value.

---

## Annualized Return (Compound Annual Growth Rate - CAGR)

To compare investments held for different lengths of time, you must calculate the **annualized return** (the geometric average return per year):

$$\text{CAGR} = \left( \frac{\text{Ending Value}}{\text{Beginning Value}} \right)^{\frac{1}{t}} - 1$$

Where $t$ is the time in years. This formula accounts for compounding and allows you to compare a 50% ROI over 5 years against a 10% ROI over 1 year.

---

## Key Terms

- **Return:** The financial gain or loss on an investment.
- **ROI:** Return on Investment; percentage gain relative to cost.
- **Capital Gain:** The profit from selling an asset at a higher price than its purchase cost.
- **Yield:** The annual cash income generated by an asset (dividends, interest).
- **CAGR:** Compound Annual Growth Rate; the annualized rate of return.
- **Real Return:** Return adjusted for inflation.
