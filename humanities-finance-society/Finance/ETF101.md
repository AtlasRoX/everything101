# ETF 101

> *An Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) is a type of pooled investment security that operates much like a mutual fund, but is bought and sold on a public stock exchange just like individual stocks.*

---

## The Big Picture

If you want the diversification of a mutual fund, but you also want the flexibility to buy and sell shares instantly at any second of the trading day, what do you buy?

You buy an **Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)**.

First introduced in 1993, ETFs have revolutionized the investing world. By taking the concept of a pooled basket of stocks and listing it on a public exchange (like the NYSE), ETFs made diversified, passive investing accessible to everyone at a fraction of the cost of traditional mutual funds.

This article introduces what an ETF is, how it compares to a mutual fund, and the benefits of passive index tracking.

---

## ETF vs. Mutual Fund: The Key Differences

While both ETFs and mutual funds represent a pooled basket of assets, they function differently in three major ways:

| Characteristic | Mutual Fund | ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) |
|---|---|---|
| **Trading Mechanics** | Priced and traded only **once a day** after market close. | Traded **intraday** on an exchange; prices fluctuate every second. |
| **Fees (Expense Ratios)** | Typically higher (often 0.5% to 1.5% for active funds). | Typically lower (often 0.03% to 0.1% for index ETFs). |
| **Tax Efficiency** | Less efficient. Internal trading by the manager triggers capital gains taxes for all shareholders. | Highly efficient. The creation/redemption mechanism minimizes capital gains events. |
| **Minimum Investment** | Often has minimums (e.g., $3,000 to start). | No minimums; you can buy a single share (often under $100). |

---

## How ETFs Work: Index Tracking

The vast majority of ETFs are **passively managed index funds**. Instead of hiring a high-priced manager to select stocks, the ETF uses an automated computer algorithm to track an existing market index:

- **S&P 500 ETF (e.g., SPY or VOO):** Tracks the 500 largest public companies in the US. By buying one share of this ETF, you own a tiny slice of Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Berkshire Hathaway, and 495 others.
- **Total Stock Market ETF (e.g., VTI):** Tracks every single public company in the US (around 4,000 stocks).
- **International Index ETF (e.g., VXUS):** Tracks international markets outside the US.

Because index tracking is automated, the management fees are near zero (Vanguard's S&P 500 ETF has an expense ratio of **0.03%**, costing only $3 a year for every $10,000 invested).

---

## Key Terms

- **ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund):** A security tracking an index or basket of assets, traded on a stock exchange.
- **Index Fund:** A passive fund designed to mimic the performance of a financial market index.
- **Intraday Trading:** Buying and selling securities within the same trading day.
- **Tax Efficiency:** The minimization of tax liabilities within an investment vehicle.
- **Expense Ratio:** The percentage of assets deducted annually for fund management.
