# Customer 101

> *Customer management is the process of acquiring, satisfying, and retaining customers to maximize the long-term value they bring to a business.*

---

## The Big Picture

Why is it that credit card companies can afford to give you $200 in free cash just for signing up? Why do video game consoles sell their hardware at a financial loss?

They do it because they understand that a customer is not just worth the money they spend on their **first transaction**. They are worth the money they will spend over the **entire duration of their relationship** with the company.

In the modern subscription- and digital-driven economy, businesses live and die by two metrics: **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** and **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**. 

If you spend more to acquire a customer than they spend with you over their lifetime, your business will go bankrupt, no matter how fast you grow.

This article introduces the calculation of LTV and CAC, the critical LTV:CAC ratio, and the concept of churn.

---

## The Two Core Metrics: LTV and CAC

To evaluate customer health, businesses track two primary metrics:

### 1. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
The total amount of money a business spends on sales and marketing to acquire a single new customer.
- **Formula:** 

$$\text{CAC} = \frac{\text{Total Sales \& Marketing Expenses}}{\text{Number of New Customers Acquired}}$$

- *Example:* If you spend $10,000 on Facebook ads and hire a marketer for $5,000 (total $15,000) and acquire 100 new customers, your CAC is **$150** per customer.

### 2. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
The total net revenue a business expects to earn from a single customer over the entire duration of their relationship with the company.
- **Formula (Simple):** 

$$\text{LTV} = \text{Average Purchase Value} \times \text{Average Number of Purchases per Year} \times \text{Average Customer Lifespan}$$

- *Example:* If a customer pays a monthly subscription of $20 and stays with your service for an average of 24 months, their LTV is **$480** ($20 × 24).

## The Unit Economics Test: The LTV:CAC Ratio

To determine if a business model is healthy and sustainable, investors look at the **LTV:CAC ratio**:

```
                       ┌─────────────────────────┐
                       │     LTV:CAC RATIO       │
                       └────────────┬────────────┘
                                    │
         ┌──────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┐
         ▼                          ▼                          ▼
┌──────────────────┐       ┌──────────────────┐       ┌──────────────────┐
│     UNDER 1:1    │       │     IDEAL 3:1    │       │    OVER 5:1      │
│ Losing money.    │       │ Healthy balance  │       │ Under-investing. │
│ Spending $150 to │       │ of growth and    │       │ Spend more on    │
│ get $100.        │       │ profitability.   │       │ marketing to grow│
└──────────────────┘       └──────────────────┘       └──────────────────┘
```

- **LTV:CAC < 1:1:** The business is losing money on every customer. It is a leaking bucket. Growing faster will only make the losses larger.
- **LTV:CAC = 3:1 (The Golden Standard):** Highly healthy. For every $1 you spend to acquire a customer, you get $3 back in value.
- **LTV:CAC > 5:1:** The business is highly profitable, but it might be growing too slowly. It should spend more on marketing to capture the market before competitors do.

## Churn Rate: The Growth Killer

If you have a high LTV, but your customers leave the company after a few weeks, your business model will collapse. This loss of customers is called **churn**.

- **Churn Rate:** The percentage of customers who stop using your product or service during a given time period.
- **Why Churn Matters:** It is far cheaper to keep an existing customer happy than it is to find a new one. A high churn rate forces you to constantly spend money on marketing just to stay in the same place.

### Retention Strategies:
- **Customer Success:** Teams dedicated to helping customers get value out of your product immediately after purchase.
- **Loyalty Programs:** Rewarding repeat customers with discounts or perks.
- **Feedback Loops:** Surveying churned customers to understand why they left, and fixing those product flaws.

---

## Key Terms

- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** The cost to convince a prospect to buy a product.
- **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV):** The total revenue a customer generates over their relationship lifetime.
- **Churn Rate:** The rate at which customers stop doing business with an entity.
- **Retention Rate:** The percentage of customers a company retains over a period.
- **Unit Economics:** The direct revenues and costs associated with an individual business model unit (a customer).
