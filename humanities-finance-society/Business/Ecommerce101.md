# Ecommerce 101

> *Ecommerce (electronic commerce) is the buying and selling of goods or services using the internet, and the transfer of money and data to execute these transactions.*

---

## The Big Picture

Historically, if you wanted to start a retail business, you had to rent a physical storefront, hire salespeople, and hope that local foot traffic would notice you. 

Today, **ecommerce** allows anyone to launch a global storefront from their laptop. You can showcase products to customers in New York, London, and Tokyo simultaneously, open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

However, while ecommerce eliminates physical barriers, it introduces a highly competitive digital landscape. Instead of competing with the shop down the street, you are competing with Amazon and thousands of global sellers. Success in ecommerce requires mastering digital marketing channels, web conversion rates, and fast shipping logistics.

This article introduces digital traffic channels, the ecommerce funnel, conversion optimization, and shipping fulfillment.

---

## Getting Traffic: PPC vs. SEO

To get customers into a digital storefront, you must drive traffic to your website. Marketers do this through two main channels:

### 1. PPC (Pay-Per-Click / Paid Search)
Paying search engines or social media platforms to display your ads to users.
- **How it works:** You pay a fee every time a user clicks on your ad (e.g., Google Ads, Facebook Ads).
- **Pros:** Instant traffic. You can target specific keywords or demographics immediately.
- **Cons:** Expensive. If you stop paying, your traffic disappears.

### 2. SEO (Search Engine Optimization / Organic Search)
Optimizing your website content and structure so search engines display your pages for free in search results.
- **How it works:** Writing high-quality product descriptions, articles, and optimizing page load speeds.
- **Pros:** Free, long-term traffic. A high rank on Google can bring customers for years.
- **Cons:** Slow. It can take months of work before you see results.

## The Ecommerce Funnel: Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

Once a user visits your website, your goal is to guide them to purchase. Economists track this using the **Ecommerce Funnel**:

```
Web Visitors (1,000)
  ▼
┌───────────────────────────────────────┐
│           PRODUCT PAGE VIEW           │ (User looks at item details)
└───────────────┬───────┬───────────────┘
                │       │
                ▼       ▼
        ┌───────────────────────┐
        │      ADD TO CART      │ (User saves item for purchase)
        └───────┬───────┬───────┘
                │       │
                ▼       ▼
            ┌───────────────┐
            │   CHECKOUT    │ (User enters shipping/payment info)
            └───────┬───────┘
                    │
                    ▼
               ┌─────────┐
               │ PURCHASE│ (Order placed; Payment processed)
               └─────────┘
                    ▼
           Paying Customers (20)
```

- **Conversion Rate:** The percentage of website visitors who make a purchase.

$$\text{Conversion Rate} = \frac{\text{Purchases}}{\text{Total Visitors}} \times 100$$

- *Example:* If 1,000 people visit your store and 20 buy, your conversion rate is **2%** (the average for modern ecommerce).
- **Shopping Cart Abandonment:** A major challenge where users add items to their cart but leave the site before paying (averages around 70%). Marketers reduce this by sending recovery emails, offering free shipping, and simplifying the checkout form.

## Fulfillment: Delivering the Goods

Once the checkout button is pressed, the digital transaction becomes a physical logistics challenge:

- **Self-Fulfillment:** The seller keeps inventory in their basement, packs the orders themselves, and drives to the post office. (Good for beginners).
- **Third-Party Logistics (3PL):** The seller rents space in a giant professional warehouse. When an order is placed on the website, the warehouse automatically packs and ships the product.
- **Dropshipping:** The seller does not buy or store any inventory. When a customer buys a product, the seller purchases the item from a third-party manufacturer, who ships it directly to the customer. (Low cost, but low profit margins and no quality control).

---

## Key Terms

- **Ecommerce:** Buying and selling goods or services over the internet.
- **PPC (Pay-Per-Click):** Paid advertising where you pay for each click on your ad.
- **SEO (Search Engine Optimization):** Optimizing website content for free search engine traffic.
- **Conversion Rate:** The percentage of visitors who complete a desired action (a purchase).
- **Shopping Cart Abandonment:** Adding products to a digital cart but leaving before checkout.
- **3PL (Third-Party Logistics):** Outsourcing warehousing and fulfillment to a specialist company.
