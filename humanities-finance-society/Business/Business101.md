# Business 101

> *A business is an organizational entity engaged in commercial, industrial, or professional activities, designed to create value and solve customer problems in exchange for a profit.*

---

## The Big Picture

Why do businesses exist? Many people would answer: *To make money.*

But making money is actually the *result* of a successful business, not its starting point. If you start a business with the sole goal of making money, but you produce nothing that anyone wants to buy, you will quickly go bankrupt.

At its core, a business exists to **solve a problem** or **satisfy a need** for a customer. A bakery solves the problem of hunger and convenience; an airline solves the problem of long-distance travel. 

A business is a machine that takes inputs (raw materials, labor, ideas) and transforms them into outputs of **higher value**. If the value created is greater than the cost of the inputs, the business earns a **profit**.

This article introduces value creation, the structure of a transaction, the difference between revenue and profit, and the role of risk.

---

## The Core Mechanism: The Exchange of Value

Every business transaction is an exchange of value between two parties:

```
                  ┌───────────────────────────────┐
                  │           BUSINESS            │
                  └──────────────┬────────────────┘
                                 │
                  ┌──────────────┴──────────────┐
                  │  Delivers Value (Product/   │
                  │  Service)                   │
                  ▼                             ▼
       ┌────────────────────┐         ┌────────────────────┐
       │   CUSTOMER NEEDS   │◄────────┤   CUSTOMER PAYS    │
       │ Solves problem or  │         │ Money (Exceeds cost│
       │ satisfies desire   │         │ to produce good)   │
       └────────────────────┘         └────────────────────┘
```

For this transaction to happen:
1. **The Customer** must value the product *more* than the money they give up.
2. **The Business** must value the money *more* than the cost of producing and delivering the product.

This mutual benefit is the foundation of all commerce.

## Profit vs. Loss: The Market's Feedback

How does a business know if it is creating value? It looks at its financial results:

- **Revenue (Top Line):** The total amount of money a business receives from selling its goods or services.
- **Expenses (Costs):** The money spent on raw materials, wages, rent, marketing, and taxes to produce those goods.
- **Profit (Bottom Line):** The financial gain when revenue exceeds expenses:

$$\text{Profit} = \text{Revenue} - \text{Expenses}$$

- **Loss:** When expenses exceed revenue, the business loses money.

In a market economy, **profit and loss are feedback loops**:
- **Profit** signals that the business is successfully transforming resources into something society values highly.
- **Loss** signals that the business is wasting resources, producing goods that people do not value enough to cover the cost of production. Persistent losses force the business to adapt or shut down, freeing up resources for better uses.

## The Role of Risk

Starting and running a business is inherently risky. An entrepreneur must spend money *today* (buying equipment, renting space, hiring workers) with no guarantee that customers will buy the product *tomorrow*.

Types of business risk include:
- **Market Risk:** Will customers want the product? Will a competitor release a cheaper alternative?
- **Financial Risk:** Will the business run out of cash before becoming profitable?
- **Operational Risk:** Will equipment break down, or will supply chains fail?

Because of these risks, **profit is the reward for taking calculated risks**. Without the incentive of profit, individuals would not risk their savings and time to build new products or services.

---

## Key Terms

- **Business:** An organization that creates and exchanges value to earn a profit.
- **Value Creation:** The process of making a product or service more valuable than the raw resources used to build it.
- **Revenue:** The total money received from sales.
- **Expenses:** The total costs incurred to run the business.
- **Profit:** Revenue minus expenses when the result is positive.
- **Risk:** The probability of financial loss or business failure.
