# Negotiation 101

> *Negotiation is a dialogue between two or more people or parties intended to reach a beneficial outcome on one or more issues where a conflict of interest exists.*

---

## The Big Picture

We negotiate every day. You negotiate with your boss for a higher salary, with a supplier for a lower price, or with your spouse on where to go for dinner. 

Yet, many people dread negotiation because they view it as a fight—a high-stress battle where one side wins and the other loses.

In modern business, negotiation is viewed as a **collaborative problem-solving process**. The goal is not to defeat the other side, but to discover terms that make both parties better off than they were before. By understanding key negotiation variables, you can approach any deal with confidence and create joint value.

This article introduces BATNA, reservation prices, the Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA), and the difference between win-lose and win-win negotiation.

---

## The Core Variables of a Negotiation

Before entering any negotiation, you must calculate three critical numbers or positions:

```
 Buyer's Walkaway                              Seller's Walkaway
      (RPb)                                         (RPs)
        ┼───────────────────────*─────────────────────┼
                                │
                        ┌───────┴───────┐
                        │   THE ZOPA    │
                        │   (Zone of    │
                        │   Possible    │
                        │  Agreement)   │
                        └───────────────┘
```

### 1. BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)
Your backup plan if the current negotiation fails completely.
- **Why it matters:** Your BATNA is your source of power. If you are negotiating for a job, and you already have another job offer in your pocket (a strong BATNA), you can walk away if the salary is too low. If you have no other job offers (a weak BATNA), you have no power and must accept their terms.

### 2. Reservation Price (Walkaway Point)
The absolute limit of what you are willing to accept or pay in a negotiation. It is directly determined by your BATNA.
- **Buyer's Reservation Price:** The maximum price you will pay. (If they charge more, you walk away).
- **Seller's Reservation Price:** The minimum price you will accept. (If they offer less, you walk away).

### 3. ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)
The overlapping range between the buyer's and seller's reservation prices.
- If the seller's minimum price is **$100**, and the buyer's maximum price is **$150**, the ZOPA is **$100 to $150**. An agreement is possible.
- If the seller's minimum price is **$200**, and the buyer's maximum price is **$150**, there is no ZOPA. No trade can happen.

## Two Styles of Negotiation

Negotiations fall into two main categories depending on the issues involved:

### 1. Distributive Negotiation (Win-Lose / "Slicing the Pie")
A negotiation over a single issue (usually price) where any gain by one party is a direct loss for the other.
- **Example:** Buying a used car. Every dollar you negotiate off the price is a dollar out of the seller's pocket.
- **Strategy:** Make the first offer to anchor the negotiation, share minimal information, and make small, decreasing concessions.

### 2. Integrative Negotiation (Win-Win / "Expanding the Pie")
A negotiation involving multiple issues (price, delivery date, contract length, warranty) where parties have different priorities.
- **Example:** A supplier negotiation. You care about price, but the supplier cares about the length of the contract. You agree to sign a longer three-year contract in exchange for a 10% discount today.
- **Strategy:** Share information openly, ask about the other side's priorities, and package multiple issues together to create joint value.

---

## Key Terms

- **Negotiation:** The process of resolving conflicting interests to reach an agreement.
- **BATNA:** Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement; your backup option.
- **Reservation Price:** Your walkaway point; the worst deal you are willing to accept.
- **ZOPA:** Zone of Possible Agreement; the range where a deal can happen.
- **Distributive Negotiation:** A win-lose negotiation over a fixed resource.
- **Integrative Negotiation:** A win-win negotiation that creates value by trading off multiple issues.
