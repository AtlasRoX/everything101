# Sales 101

> *Sales is the process of helping a customer make a buying decision that solves their problem, resulting in a transaction.*

---

## The Big Picture

What is the difference between marketing and sales?
- **Marketing** is about getting people interested in your product. It builds the brand, identifies target segments, and attracts leads.
- **Sales** is about closing the deal. It is the direct interaction (whether in-person, over the phone, or via a digital interface) that turns that interested lead into a paying customer.

Historically, salespeople were viewed as aggressive persuaders ("slick car salesmen"). 

But modern sales is a science of **consultative selling**. It is about asking questions, listening to customer frustrations, and showing how your product can solve their problem. If your product does not help the customer, a good salesperson will walk away, because forcing a sale leads to angry customers, returns, and brand damage.

This article introduces the sales funnel, the difference between B2B and B2C sales, and pipeline management.

---

## The Sales Funnel: The Customer Journey

To manage sales, companies use a framework called the **Sales Funnel** (or AIDA model). The funnel is wide at the top (many prospects) and narrow at the bottom (few closed deals):

```
Prospects
  ▼
┌───────────────────────────────────────┐
│              AWARENESS                │ (Prospect learns about the product)
└───────────────┬───────┬───────────────┘
                │       │
                ▼       ▼
        ┌───────────────────────┐
        │       INTEREST        │ (Prospect researches & evaluates features)
        └───────┬───────┬───────┘
                │       │
                ▼       ▼
            ┌───────────────┐
            │   DECISION    │ (Prospect compares pricing & options)
            └───────┬───────┘
                    │
                    ▼
               ┌─────────┐
               │ ACTION  │ (Prospect signs contract / pays)
               └─────────┘
                    ▼
                Customers
```

- **Awareness:** A prospect visits your website, reads an ad, or gets a cold email.
- **Interest:** The prospect requests a product demo, signs up for a newsletter, or asks for more information.
- **Decision:** The prospect compares your pricing and features with your competitors. The sales representative addresses their objections.
- **Action (Closing):** The contract is signed, the invoice is paid, and the prospect becomes a customer.

## B2B vs. B2C Sales: Different Worlds

Sales approaches differ significantly depending on who you are selling to:

| Characteristic | B2C Sales (Business-to-Consumer) | B2B Sales (Business-to-Business) |
|---|---|---|
| **Buyer** | Individual or family. | Business representatives, committees, procurement departments. |
| **Sales Cycle** | Short (minutes to days). | Long (months to over a year). |
| **Decision Driver** | Emotional, convenience, price. | Rational, return on investment (ROI), efficiency. |
| **Value / Price** | Low to moderate. | Very High (contracts worth millions). |
| **Example** | Buying shoes, ordering food online. | Selling enterprise software to a bank. |

In **B2B sales**, relationship selling is vital. The salesperson acts as a trusted advisor, working closely with the client's internal team to customize the solution and navigate corporate approvals.

## Pipeline Management: The Sales Engine

To hit revenue goals, sales managers track a **sales pipeline**—the active deals their team is working on. Key metrics include:

- **Lead Velocity:** How fast new leads are entering the funnel.
- **Conversion Rate:** The percentage of leads that move from one stage of the funnel to the next.
- **Win Rate:** The percentage of total leads that successfully close as paying customers.
- **Average Deal Size:** The average contract value of a closed sale.

---

## Key Terms

- **Sales:** The activities involved in selling goods or services.
- **Sales Funnel:** The visual model of a customer's journey from awareness to purchase.
- **B2B (Business-to-Business):** Transactions between businesses (e.g., manufacturer to wholesaler).
- **B2C (Business-to-Consumer):** Transactions directly between a business and individual consumers.
- **Prospect (Lead):** A person or organization that has shown interest in your product but has not purchased yet.
- **Objection:** A concern or hesitation raised by a prospect during the sales process (e.g., "It's too expensive").
