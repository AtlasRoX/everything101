# Leadership 101

> *Leadership is the art of motivating a group of people to act toward achieving a common goal. It is about vision, alignment, and inspiration.*

---

## The Big Picture

Are managers and leaders the same thing?
- A **manager** focuses on systems, structure, budgets, and control. They ask: *"How can we do this efficiently?"*
- A **leader** focuses on people, vision, alignment, and motivation. They ask: *"Why are we doing this, and where are we going?"*

In any organization, you need both:
- Management keeps the trains running on time.
- Leadership decides which direction the tracks should be laid.

Leadership is not about a formal title. You can have a CEO who is a bad leader, and a junior employee who exhibits incredible leadership by rallying colleagues to solve a difficult problem. Leadership is the ability to influence others without relying solely on formal authority.

This article introduces the difference between management and leadership, common leadership styles, and the concept of emotional intelligence.

## Authority vs. Influence: The Source of Power

Where does a leader's power come from? French and Raven identified five main sources of power, which can be grouped into two types:

```
                            ┌─────────────────────────┐
                            │    SOURCES OF POWER     │
                            └────────────┬────────────┘
                                         │
         ┌───────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────┐
         ▼                                                               ▼
┌──────────────────┐                                            ┌──────────────────┐
│  POSITION POWER  │                                            │  PERSONAL POWER  │
│ (Authority/Title)│                                            │(Influence/Respect│
│ - Legitimate     │                                            │ - Expert         │
│ - Reward         │                                            │ - Referent       │
│ - Coercive       │                                            │                  │
└──────────────────┘                                            └──────────────────┘
```

### 1. Position Power (Formal Authority)
- **Legitimate Power:** Power derived from your formal job title (e.g., "I am the boss, so do it").
- **Reward Power:** The power to give raises, promotions, or praise.
- **Coercive Power:** The power to fire, demote, or reprimand.

### 2. Personal Power (True Influence)
- **Expert Power:** Power derived from having deep skills, knowledge, or expertise that others respect.
- **Referent Power:** Power derived from interpersonal respect, charisma, and trust. People follow you because they admire you and want to work with you.

> **Aha! moment:** Great leaders rely primarily on **personal power** (expert and referent). If you must constantly threaten employees with firing (coercive) or demand compliance because of your title (legitimate), you are a boss, not a leader.

## Four Leadership Styles

Different situations require different styles of leadership. Psychologists identify four main approaches:

- **Autocratic (Authoritarian):** The leader makes all decisions unilaterally without consulting the team. (Useful in emergencies or when the team is untrained, but destroys long-term morale).
- **Democratic (Participative):** The leader consults the team and encourages collaboration before making a decision. (Builds high commitment and innovative ideas, but can be slow).
- **Laissez-Faire (Delegative):** The leader gives the team complete freedom to make decisions and complete tasks. (Works well with highly skilled, self-motivated experts, but leads to chaos if the team lacks direction).
- **Transformational:** The leader inspires the team with a compelling vision, coaching them to grow and achieve more than they thought possible.

## Emotional Intelligence (EQ) in Leadership

Psychologist Daniel Goleman argued that the best leaders possess high **Emotional Intelligence (EQ)**—the ability to identify, understand, and manage emotions in themselves and others. EQ has four pillars:
1. **Self-Awareness:** Understanding your own strengths, weaknesses, and emotional triggers.
2. **Self-Management:** Controlling impulsive emotions and adapting to change.
3. **Social Awareness (Empathy):** Understanding the feelings and viewpoints of your team members.
4. **Relationship Management:** Inspiring others, managing conflict, and building strong bonds.

---

## Key Terms

- **Leadership:** The process of directing and influencing others to achieve a goal.
- **Influence:** The ability to alter other people's behavior without force.
- **Emotional Intelligence (EQ):** The ability to manage self and social emotions.
- **Democratic Style:** Participative decision-making leadership.
- **Transformational Leadership:** Leadership that inspires positive changes in subordinates.
