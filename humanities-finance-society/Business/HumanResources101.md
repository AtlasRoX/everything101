# Human Resources 101

> *Human Resources (HR) is the department within a business that is responsible for all things related to employers, including recruiting, vetting, selecting, hiring, onboarding, training, promoting, paying, and firing employees.*

---

## The Big Picture

A company's factories, machinery, and software are useless without people to operate them. The collective skills, energy, and intelligence of a company's employees are its most valuable asset—its **human capital**. (See [Labor101](../Economics/Labor101.md)).

**Human Resources (HR)** is the management of this capital. 

HR is not just about paperwork, payroll, and resolving disputes. At its best, HR is a strategic partner that helps a business win by hiring the best talent, training them to perform at their peak, building a healthy workplace culture, and keeping the company out of legal trouble.

This article introduces the employee lifecycle, performance management, and the concept of company culture.

---

## The Employee Lifecycle

HR manages the journey of an employee through the company, divided into five main stages:

```
┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐
│ 1. RECRUIT  │───►│ 2. ONBOARD  │───►│  3. DEVELOP │───►│  4. RETAIN  │───►│ 5. OFFBOARD │
│ Find & hire │    │ Setup tools │    │ Training &  │    │ Benefits,   │    │ Exit interviews│
│ the talent  │    │ & culture   │    │ performance │    │ promotions  │    │ & compliance│
└─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘
```

1. **Recruitment & Selection:** Writing job descriptions, screening resumes, conducting interviews, and making job offers.
2. **Onboarding:** Welcoming the new hire, setting up their computer and email, introducing them to the team, and helping them understand company rules.
3. **Training & Development:** Teaching employees new skills so they can do their jobs better and prepare for promotions.
4. **Retention:** Keeping top performers happy so they don't leave for competitors (using compensation, flexible hours, health benefits, and recognition).
5. **Separation (Offboarding):** Managing resignations, retirements, or firings. Conducting exit interviews to learn how the company can improve, and ensuring security access is revoked.

## Performance Management: The Review Cycle

To ensure employees are aligned with company goals, HR designs the **performance management cycle**:

- **Goal Setting:** At the start of the year, employees and managers agree on specific, measurable goals (often using the SMART framework: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
- **Performance Appraisals (Reviews):** Periodic evaluations where managers assess an employee's output, provide feedback, and determine raises or promotions.
- **360-Degree Feedback:** A modern review method where feedback is gathered not just from the manager, but also from the employee's peers, direct reports, and even clients, providing a well-rounded picture.

## Company Culture: The Invisible Rules

Why do people love working at some companies and dread working at others? The difference is **company culture**.

> **Company Culture** is the shared values, beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors that characterize an organization and guide its practices.

- **Strong Culture:** Employees understand and buy into the company's mission (e.g., Netflix's focus on "freedom and responsibility"). This leads to high engagement, collaboration, and trust.
- **Toxic Culture:** Marked by fear, micromanagement, lack of transparency, and gossip, leading to high employee turnover (people quitting) and low productivity.

HR shapes culture by defining company values, rewarding positive behavior, and hiring people who align with those values.

---

## Key Terms

- **Human Resources:** The department managing a company's workforce.
- **Human Capital:** The collective skills, knowledge, and health of employees.
- **Onboarding:** The process of integrating a new employee into the company.
- **Performance Appraisal:** The evaluation of employee job performance.
- **360-Degree Feedback:** Performance feedback gathered from all levels around an employee.
- **Company Culture:** The shared values and behaviors of an organization.
