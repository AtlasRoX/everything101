# Marketing 101

> *Marketing is the activity, set of institutions, and processes for creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large.*

---

## The Big Picture

Many people think **marketing** is just a fancy word for advertising. 
- But advertising is only a small slice of the marketing pie. 
- If you build a product that nobody wants, even the most beautiful, expensive advertisement in the world will not make you successful.

Marketing is the bridge between a business and its customers. It is the science of understanding what customers need, designing a product that meets those needs, setting a price they are willing to pay, choosing where to sell it, and communicating its benefits. 

As the marketing legend Peter Drucker wrote: **"The aim of marketing is to make selling superfluous. The aim of marketing is to know and understand the customer so well that the product or service fits him and sells itself."**

This article introduces the 4 Ps of marketing, the STP framework (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning), and market research.

## The 4 Ps of the Marketing Mix

To execute a marketing strategy, managers adjust four primary variables, known as the **Marketing Mix**:

```
                       ┌─────────────────────────┐
                       │    THE MARKETING MIX    │
                       └────────────┬────────────┘
                                    │
         ┌──────────────┬───────────┴───────────┬──────────────┐
         ▼              ▼                       ▼              ▼
┌────────────────┐┌────────────────┐┌────────────────┐┌────────────────┐
│  PRODUCT (1)   ││   PRICE (2)    ││   PLACE (3)    ││ PROMOTION (4)  │
│ What is sold?  ││ What it costs? ││ Where is it    ││ How do buyers  │
│ Features, design││ Value based,   ││ sold? Channels││ find out? Ads, │
│ quality, packaging│ cost-plus, discount│ retail, online │ sales, PR      │
└────────────────┘└────────────────┘└────────────────┘└────────────────┘
```

1. **Product:** The physical good or intangible service you sell. Includes design, features, quality, brand name, and packaging. (Does it solve the customer's problem?).
2. **Price:** The amount of money customers must pay to get the product. (How does price signal quality? Should you use a subscription model, discount model, or premium model?). (See [Price101](../Economics/Price101.md)).
3. **Place:** The distribution channels where the product is sold. (Do you sell online directly to consumers, in high-end boutiques, or on mass retail shelves like Walmart?).
4. **Promotion:** The communication activities used to inform and persuade customers. Includes advertising, public relations (PR), social media marketing, and discount coupons.

## The STP Framework: Focus Your Fire

A business cannot be all things to all people. If you try to sell to "everyone," your message will be too generic to appeal to anyone. Marketing solves this using the **STP Framework**:

### 1. Segmentation (Divide)
Dividing the massive market of consumers into smaller, distinct groups based on shared characteristics:
- **Demographics:** Age, income, gender, education.
- **Geographics:** Country, city, climate.
- **Psychographics:** Lifestyle, values, interests.
- **Behavioral:** Usage rates, brand loyalty.

### 2. Targeting (Select)
Evaluating the segments and choosing which ones your business is best equipped to serve.
- *Example:* Instead of targeting "all runners," a company might target "high-income trail runners."

### 3. Positioning (Differentiate)
Designing your product offering and image so that it occupies a distinctive, valued place in the target customer's mind relative to competitors.
- *Example:* Volvo positions itself around "safety"; Tesla positions itself around "innovation and clean energy."

## Market Research: Listening to the Market

To make smart STP decisions, marketers conduct **market research**:
- **Primary Research:** Gathering new data directly from customers (e.g., surveys, interviews, focus groups).
- **Secondary Research:** Analyzing existing data compiled by others (e.g., industry reports, census data, competitor websites).

---

## Key Terms

- **Marketing:** The process of creating, communicating, and delivering value to customers.
- **Marketing Mix (4 Ps):** Product, Price, Place, and Promotion.
- **Segmentation:** Dividing a market into distinct groups of buyers.
- **Targeting:** Selecting which market segments to focus on.
- **Positioning:** Establishing a distinct brand identity in the minds of target customers.
- **Primary Research:** Collecting fresh, proprietary data directly from subjects.
