# Decolonization 101: The Rise of the Global South 🌍

In 1945, the map of the world was dominated by European empires. Great Britain, France, Belgium, and the Netherlands ruled over half of the globe. 

By 1975, those empires had almost completely disappeared. 

Over a hundred new nations emerged, raising new flags, joining the United Nations, and redefining global politics. This process is called **Decolonization**—the dismantling of colonial empires and the transition of colonies into independent, self-governing states. It was one of the largest transfers of political power in human history, creating what we now call the "Global South" or the "Third World."

---

## Why Decolonization Happened: The Perfect Storm 🌪️

Decolonization was not a gift from the colonizers; it was a result of three converging pressures:

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│             1. WEAKENED EMPIRES                        │
│  - Britain, France, and Belgium are bankrupt post-WWII │
└───────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                            │  +
┌───────────────────────────▼────────────────────────────┐
│             2. POPULAR NATIONALIST MOVEMENTS           │
│  - Local populations mobilize, strike, and rebel       │
└───────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                            │  +
┌───────────────────────────▼────────────────────────────┐
│             3. COLD WAR PRESSURE                       │
│  - US and USSR pressure empires to open markets/allies │
└───────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                            │  =
┌───────────────────────────▼────────────────────────────┐
│             DECOLONIZATION & INDEPENDENCE              │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

1.  **Imperial Bankruptcy:** World War II left European nations financially ruined and militarily exhausted. They could no longer afford the armies needed to police distant colonies.
2.  **Nationalist Mobilization:** Educated local elites (many of whom had studied in Europe) organized political parties, newspapers, labor unions, and armies to demand self-rule.
3.  **Superpower Rivalry:** Both the United States and the Soviet Union wanted to dismantle European empires to gain access to new markets and draw new independent nations into their Cold War alliances.

---

## Paths to Independence: Violent vs. Non-Violent 🛠_

Independent movements took different paths depending on how desperately the colonizer wanted to hold onto the territory:

### 1. The Non-Violent Path (Negotiated Exit)
Through civil disobedience, strikes, and political pressure, colonies made it too expensive or inconvenient for the colonizer to stay.
*   **Example: India (1947):** Led by Mahatma Gandhi, the Indian independence movement used non-violent resistance (*Satyagraha*). In 1947, Britain agreed to leave, but partitioned the subcontinent into Hindu-majority India and Muslim-majority Pakistan, sparking massive displacement and violence.
*   **Example: Ghana (1957):** Kwame Nkrumah led a strike campaign that forced Britain to grant Ghana independence, launching the wave of decolonization in Sub-Saharan Africa.

### 2. The Violent Path (War of Liberation)
When colonizing powers (especially those with permanent white settler populations) refused to negotiate, local movements launched guerrilla wars.
*   **Example: Algeria (1954–1962):** France viewed Algeria not as a colony, but as a province of France. The FLN (National Liberation Front) launched a bloody guerrilla war that eventually forced France to withdraw.
*   **Example: Vietnam (1945–1954):** Communist leader Ho Chi Minh defeated the French army at the Battle of Dien Bien Phu, ending French rule in Indochina.

---

## The Non-Aligned Movement: The "Third World" 🏳️

During the Cold War, the United States was the "First World," and the Soviet Union was the "Second World." 

To avoid being dragged into this superpower chess game, leaders of newly independent nations (like Nehru of India, Nkrumah of Ghana, and Sukarno of Indonesia) met at the **Bandung Conference** in 1955. They formed the **Non-Aligned Movement**—the "Third World"—arguing that they should focus on economic development and sovereignty rather than taking sides in the Cold War.

---

## Why Decolonization Matters Today

*   **The UN General Assembly:** Decolonization transformed the United Nations. In 1945, the UN had 51 member states. Today, it has 193, with the majority representing post-colonial nations.
*   **Post-Colonial Instability:** The artificial borders drawn by European empires remain. When colonizers exited, they often left behind divided communities and weak institutions, resulting in civil wars, military coups, and ethnic conflicts.
