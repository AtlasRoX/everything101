# Communism 101

> *Communism is a revolutionary political and economic ideology that aims to establish a classless, stateless, and moneyless society based on common ownership of the means of production.*

---

## The Big Picture

While social democrats want to reform capitalism and democratic socialists want to vote it out of office, **communists** argue that capitalism is so deeply exploitative that it cannot be fixed. They believe the capitalist class (the owners) will never willingly give up their power, and therefore, a **working-class revolution** is necessary to build a new society.

Historically, communist revolutions in the 20th century (in Russia, China, and Cuba) reshaped global politics, leading to the Cold War. However, the systems that arose under leaders like Joseph Stalin and Mao Zedong differed significantly from the stateless, egalitarian society envisioned by Karl Marx.

This article introduces the core theories of communism, the steps to achieving it, and how it was implemented in history.

---

## Marx’s Core Theories

Modern communism is based on the writings of **Karl Marx (1818–1883)** and **Friedrich Engels (1820–1895)**. Their philosophy is built on two key concepts:

### 1. Historical Materialism
Marx argued that human history is driven by **economics** (material conditions). How we produce food, clothes, and shelter determines our culture, laws, religion, and political systems. History progresses through stages (feudalism → capitalism → socialism → communism) as our methods of production evolve.

### 2. Class Struggle
In every historical era, society has been split into those who own the resources and those who do the work:
- **The Bourgeoisie (Capitalists):** The small minority who own the factories, land, and machinery (the means of production).
- **The Proletariat (Workers):** The vast majority who own nothing but their ability to work, which they must sell to the capitalists for a wage.

Marx argued that capitalists make a profit by paying workers less than the actual value of their labor (exploitation). This creates an inevitable conflict that will eventually lead to the collapse of capitalism.

## The Path to Communism: Marx's Stages

Marx did not think a classless society could appear overnight. He outlined a transition:

```
┌──────────────────┐      ┌─────────────────────────┐      ┌──────────────────┐
│   CAPITALISM     │─────>│       SOCIALISM         │─────>│    COMMUNISM     │
│Private ownership;│      │ Dictatorship of the     │      │ Classless, state-│
│class divisions & │      │ Proletariat: State runs │      │ less, moneyless  │
│exploitation      │      │ economy for workers     │      │ society; common  │
└──────────────────┘      └─────────────────────────┘      │ ownership        │
                                                           └──────────────────┘
```

1. **Revolution:** The workers (proletariat) overthrow the capitalist class in a revolution.
2. **Socialism (The Transitional State):** The workers establish the **"dictatorship of the proletariat"**—using the power of the state to seize all private property, nationalize industries, and distribute resources based on work. (The state still exists to protect the revolution from capitalist counter-attacks).
3. **Communism (The Final Stage):** As class divisions disappear, the state becomes unnecessary. Without classes, there is no need for one group to oppress another, so the state **"withers away."** The final society is stateless, moneyless, and runs on the principle: **"From each according to his ability, to each according to his needs."**

## Historical Implementation and the Soviet Model

In 1917, **Vladimir Lenin** led the Bolshevik Revolution in Russia, establishing the world's first state claiming to follow Marx's ideas (the Soviet Union).

However, the Soviet Union and other 20th-century communist regimes diverged from Marx's vision in several ways:
- **The Vanguard Party:** Lenin argued that ordinary workers lacked the time or education to launch a revolution on their own. Instead, a highly disciplined "vanguard party" of professional revolutionaries had to seize power and run the state on their behalf.
- **Totalitarian Control:** Instead of the state "withering away," the ruling Communist Parties built massive, centralized states that controlled all political, social, and economic life, ruthlessly crushing any opposition (e.g., under Stalin or Mao).
- **Command Economy:** The state owned all land and factories, deciding exactly what should be produced, at what price, and in what quantity. While this allowed rapid industrialization, it often led to inefficiencies, shortages, and economic stagnation.

---

## Key Terms

- **Communism:** A stateless, classless society based on common ownership of resources.
- **Bourgeoisie:** The capitalist class who own the means of production.
- **Proletariat:** The working class who must sell their labor for wages.
- **Historical Materialism:** The theory that economic forces drive historical change.
- **Dictatorship of the Proletariat:** The transitional socialist state run by the working class to dismantle capitalism.
- **Command Economy:** An economic system where the state controls all production and pricing.
