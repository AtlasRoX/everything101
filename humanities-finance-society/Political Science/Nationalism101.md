# Nationalism 101

> *Nationalism is an ideology and political movement asserting that the nation should be the fundamental unit of human social life, and that each nation has a right to govern itself independently.*

---

## The Big Picture

Why is the map of the world divided into nearly 200 sovereign countries, each with its own flag, anthem, passport, and border control? Why do we feel a sense of loyalty to millions of strangers just because they share our nationality?

The answer is **nationalism**. While it feels natural to live in a "nation-state," this is actually a relatively modern invention. For most of history, people identified with their local village, religion, or empire, rather than a nation. Rulers governed vast empires containing dozens of different cultures and languages.

Nationalism redrew the map of the world by declaring a simple, revolutionary rule: **every nation should have its own state, and every state should represent only one nation.**

---

## Nation vs. State: What's the Difference?

In everyday language, we use "nation," "state," and "country" as synonyms. In political science, they have distinct meanings:

- **The State:** A political and legal entity with a defined territory, a government, and sovereignty (the physical machinery).
- **The Nation:** A cultural and psychological entity—a group of people who believe they share a common identity, history, language, culture, and destiny (the human community).
- **The Nation-State:** A state that rules over a population that shares a single national identity (e.g., Japan, Iceland).

## Civic vs. Ethnic Nationalism

How does someone become a member of a nation? The answer divides nationalism into two major branches:

### 1. Civic (Liberal) Nationalism
National identity is defined by **political principles and citizenship**. Anyone can join the nation by agreeing to respect its laws and democratic values, regardless of their ethnicity or birthplace.
- **Key Focus:** Shared political ideals, constitution, and laws.
- **Examples:** France, the United States, Canada.

### 2. Ethnic (Cultural) Nationalism
National identity is defined by **ancestry, language, and heritage**. You are born into the nation; it is inherited from your parents, and it is very difficult for outsiders to fully integrate.
- **Key Focus:** Shared bloodlines, historical ancestry, and native language.
- **Examples:** Historical Germany (romantic nationalism), or nations built on ethnic ties.

---

## Patriotism vs. Nationalism

While the terms are often used interchangeably, many political theorists make a clear distinction:

- **Patriotism** is affection for one's country and pride in its values and achievements, without the need to denigrate other countries. It is a defensive feeling: *I love my home.*
- **Nationalism** is the belief that one's nation is superior to others, has special historical rights, and must prioritize its interests above all global cooperation. It is often exclusive: *My nation first, and others are threats.*

---

## Key Terms

- **Nationalism:** The ideology that the nation should govern itself independently and be the primary focus of political loyalty.
- **Nation:** A community of people bound by a shared identity, culture, and history.
- **Nation-State:** A sovereign country whose citizens share a single national identity.
- **Stateless Nation:** A group of people with a shared national identity who do not have their own independent state.
- **Self-Determination:** The right of a nation of people to choose their own government and political status.
