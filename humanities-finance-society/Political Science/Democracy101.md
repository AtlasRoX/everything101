# Democracy 101

> *Democracy is a system of government where power is held by the citizens, either directly or through elected representatives.*

---

## The Big Picture

The word democracy comes from the Greek words **"demos"** (people) and **"kratos"** (power or rule). Put simply, it means **"rule by the people."**

Before the spread of democracy, most societies were ruled by kings, emperors, or warlords. If a ruler was bad, the only way to replace them was through a bloody rebellion or assassination. 

Democracy introduced a revolutionary concept: **peaceful political change**. In a democracy, citizens hold the ultimate power because they can vote their leaders out of office without firing a single shot. 

This article covers the different types of democracy, the essential pillars that make a democracy work, and why democracies rarely go to war with one another.

---

## Direct vs. Representative Democracy

There are two primary ways citizens can exercise their power:

### 1. Direct Democracy
Citizens participate directly in making decisions and passing laws.
- **Example:** In ancient Athens, all male citizens gathered in an assembly to debate and vote on laws, taxes, and war.
- **Modern use:** No country is a pure direct democracy today because it is impractical for millions of people to vote on every single decision. However, many democracies use direct elements like **referendums** (where citizens vote directly on a specific law, like Brexit).

### 2. Representative Democracy (Republic)
Citizens elect representatives (politicians) to make decisions and pass laws on their behalf.
- **Example:** The United States, India, Germany, and most modern democracies.
- Representatives are held accountable through regular, free, and fair elections. If they do a poor job, citizens can vote for someone else in the next election.

## The Pillars of a Liberal Democracy

Holding elections is not enough to make a country a democracy. A country where elections are held but opposition candidates are jailed or the media is controlled by the state is an **illiberal democracy** (or electoral authoritarianism).

For a country to be a true **liberal democracy**, it must have several pillars:

1. **Free and Fair Elections:** Elections must occur regularly, all adults must have the right to vote, votes must be cast by secret ballot, and multiple political parties must be allowed to campaign freely.
2. **Protection of Individual Rights:** The majority cannot vote to take away the basic rights of a minority group. A democracy must protect freedom of speech, assembly, religion, and the press.
3. **The Rule of Law:** Rulers are bound by the constitution and ordinary laws just like everyone else.
4. **Active Civil Society:** Citizens must be able to organize, join interest groups, protest, and debate issues outside of government control.
5. **An Independent Media:** A free press that can critique the government and report news without censorship, allowing voters to make informed decisions.

## Democratic Peace Theory

One of the most famous findings in international relations is the **Democratic Peace Theory**:

> **Aha! moment:** While democracies frequently fight wars against non-democracies, **democracies almost never go to war with other democracies.**

Political scientists offer two explanations for this peace:
- **Institutional Constraints:** Democratic leaders are accountable to voters, who bear the cost of war (taxes, loss of life). Therefore, democratic leaders face high political costs if they launch a war, making them more cautious.
- **Shared Norms:** Democracies are used to settling disputes internally through compromise, debate, and legal systems. They apply these same peaceful norms when negotiating with other democracies, rather than resorting to violence.

---

## Key Terms

- **Democracy:** A system of government where the citizens hold political power.
- **Representative Democracy:** A system where citizens elect leaders to make laws on their behalf.
- **Direct Democracy:** A system where citizens vote directly on laws and policies.
- **Illiberal Democracy:** A state that holds elections but lacks the protection of rights, rule of law, and free media of a liberal democracy.
- **Tyranny of the Majority:** The risk in democracies that the majority group will vote to oppress or take rights away from a minority group.
