# Fascism 101

> *Fascism is a far-right, authoritarian, ultranationalist political ideology characterized by dictatorial power, militarism, and the complete subordination of individual interests to the state.*

---

## The Big Picture

What happens when a society rejects both individual liberty (liberalism) and working-class solidarity (socialism)? 

In the chaos following World War I, a new ideology emerged in Europe that offered a third option: **fascism**. Named after the Roman *fasces* (a bundle of rods bound around an axe, symbolizing strength through unity), fascism promised to restore national pride, order, and strength by uniting all classes under a single, all-powerful leader.

Fascism rejects democracy as weak and dividing, and rejects liberalism as selfish. Instead, it promotes constant struggle, national rebirth, and the absolute supremacy of the state.

---

## Core Pillars of Fascism

Fascism is often described as an "anti-ideology" because it defines itself by what it opposes: it is anti-democratic, anti-liberal, anti-communist, and anti-individualist. However, it is built on several key pillars:

### 1. Ultranationalism and National Rebirth (Palingenesis)
Fascists believe their nation is superior to all others and is undergoing a period of moral and cultural decay. They promise a "palingenesis"—a national rebirth or resurrection—to return the nation to its historical greatness.

### 2. Totalitarianism: "Everything in the State"
Coined by Italian dictator Benito Mussolini: **"Everything in the State, nothing outside the State, nothing against the State."** The state has the right to control all aspects of life, including the economy, education, media, and private thought. Individual rights do not exist; you only have value in how you serve the state.

### 3. The Leadership Principle (Führerprinzip)
Fascism relies on a charismatic, all-powerful dictator whose word is law. The leader is presented as the embodiment of the nation's will, and their authority is unquestionable. This is supported by a **cult of personality** built through propaganda and mass rallies.

### 4. Militarism and Social Darwinism
Fascists view life as a Darwinian struggle between nations or races, where only the strongest survive. Peace is seen as a sign of weakness and decay; war and conquest are praised as the ultimate expression of human vitality and national strength.

## The Economy Under Fascism: Corporatism

Fascists reject both free-market capitalism (for being too individualistic) and communism (for promoting class war). Instead, they advocate for **corporatism**:

```
                       ┌─────────────────────────┐
                       │     FASCIST STATE       │
                       │ (Directs national goals)│
                       └────────────┬────────────┘
                                    │
         ┌──────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────┐
         ▼                                                     ▼
┌──────────────────┐                                  ┌──────────────────┐
│ CAPITALIST OWNERS│<────────────────────────────────>│     WORKERS      │
│ Private profit   │       No strikes allowed;        │ Wages & labor;   │
│ allowed; Must    │     Must cooperate to meet       │ Must serve state │
│ serve state goals│       national production        │ goals            │
└──────────────────┘                                  └──────────────────┘
```

- Private property and corporate profits are allowed, but businesses are organized into state-directed syndicates.
- Striking is banned, trade unions are abolished, and both owners and workers are forced to cooperate to meet the state's economic and military goals.
- If a business owner does not cooperate, the state will seize their property or replace them.

## Historical Examples and the Split

Fascism dominated interwar Europe, taking two primary forms:

- **Italian Fascism (Mussolini):** Focused on state supremacy and national power. Mussolini sought to recreate the glory of the Roman Empire, prioritizing the state above all else.
- **German Nazism (Hitler):** An extreme, racialized variation of fascism. Nazism prioritized **race** over the state, viewing history not as a struggle between states, but between biological races. It led to extreme anti-Semitism, aggressive expansionism, and the Holocaust.

---

## Key Terms

- **Fascism:** An ultranationalist, dictatorial, and militaristic ideology.
- **Totalitarianism:** A system where the state has total control over public and private life.
- **Corporatism:** An economic system where the state coordinates private capital and labor in syndicates.
- **Social Darwinism:** The misapplication of evolutionary theory to human societies, suggesting that strong nations are justified in conquering weak ones.
- **Cult of Personality:** The intense devotion to and promotion of a leader through state media and propaganda.
